# Bus Out! — Playtesting Notes

## Playtest Method

Since this is an early game design case study, playtesting was conducted through paper-based testing.

The level layouts were reviewed as simple diagrams and rule descriptions. The goal was to check whether the rules were understandable, the difficulty progression was logical, and the player actions were clear.

## Playtest Goals

The playtest focused on:

- Rule clarity
- Player understanding
- Difficulty progression
- Level fairness
- Possible confusion points
- Player decision-making

---

## Playtest 1 — Rule Clarity

### Observation

The player understood that buses and passengers should match by color.

However, the player was unsure whether moving a bus without matching passengers was allowed.

### Issue

The movement rule was not clear enough.

### Improvement

Added a rule explaining that buses can move if their path is clear, but they only pick up passengers of the same color.

---

## Playtest 2 — Parking Slot Confusion

### Observation

The player understood color matching but was confused about the purpose of temporary parking slots.

### Issue

The parking slot mechanic appeared too suddenly in Level 3.

### Improvement

Added a short tutorial hint before Level 3:

"Use the parking slot to move a bus aside and open a path."

---

## Playtest 3 — Difficulty Progression

### Observation

Level 4 felt more complex because of mixed passengers and obstacles.

The player needed more time to decide the first move.

### Issue

The level may feel difficult if too many new elements appear at once.

### Improvement

Reduced the number of obstacles in Level 4 and moved the harder version to Level 5.

---

## Playtest Summary

The core rules are understandable when introduced step by step.

The most important design improvements are:

- Add clear tutorial hints.
- Introduce only one new mechanic at a time.
- Use visual feedback for blocked paths and wrong moves.
- Make early levels simple and satisfying.
- Increase difficulty through planning, not confusing layouts.
